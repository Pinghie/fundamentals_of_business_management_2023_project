Inventory Estimator Tool

This tool empowers companies to easily estimate the value and track the stock levels of their inventory. With just a few inputs about the inventory and its products, the software takes care of the rest. Real-time solutions are conveniently presented in the sidebar, offering immediate insights for effective decision-making.

Current stock

This section is specifically designed for gathering essential information on the current inventory status and formulating estimates of its present value and stock level.

Base information

To begin, we require two essential pieces of information: Maximum Inventory Capacity (which can be specified in any preferred unit of measurement, such as slots, boxes, units, etc.) and the Number of different products.


Products information

For each distinct product, it is required to know the number of pieces currently present in the inventory, the estimated selling price for each piece, the transactions (production and purchase) costs and how much space is occupied by each piece. Providing these information, the first estimates can be formulated.
In particular, considering the selling price and the transaction costs, for each product a Contribution margin is computed. The estimated inventory value depends on these product costs.

Contribution margin for each product (estimated product cost)

Predicted Stock

This section is dedicated to providing projections for future inventory value and stock levels based on selected transaction strategies. Specifically, it enables an analysis of how inventory parameters fluctuate in response to specific selling, producing, or purchasing decisions. It's also considered the variation of selling price of goods.

Price variation and product transactions

It is possible to simulate the effects of a variation in the selling price of products. Furthermore, for each distinct product it is possible to define desired transactions and observe their outcomes.

This mockup should be viewed strictly as an aid for a preliminary low-level inventory estimation. Numerous other factors, which are not addressed here, significantly contribute to determining the actual value and stock levels of an inventory. Nevertheless, this tool can offer valuable insights into initial challenges in inventory management. Additionally, it is crucial to recognize that each company possesses unique needs and complications, necessitating tailored and specific solutions.
